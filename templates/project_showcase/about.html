This project, Single Image 3D Reconstruction, focuses on generating detailed three-dimensional models from a single two-dimensional image using recent advances in generative modeling and feedforward 3D editing.

Traditional 3D reconstruction relies on multiple calibrated images or depth sensors, which limits scalability and accessibility. Our approach integrates diffusion-based priors, camera-conditioned score distillation, 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS), and feedforward 3D editing capabilities to address these challenges.

The pipeline begins with monocular depth estimation and importance-sampled point cloud initialization, followed by iterative optimization guided by a camera-aware diffusion process. Additionally, we introduce a feedforward 3D editing pipeline combining Qwen2509 with Hunyuan3D-1.0 for direct text-guided and image-guided 3D asset generation and editing.

Unlike traditional approaches that generate multi-view images and reconstruct geometry, our feedforward pipeline directly edits complete 3D assets in a single pass. This integrated framework balances geometric fidelity, computational efficiency, real-time rendering capabilities, and flexible post-reconstruction editing.

By achieving high-quality reconstructions and edits within minutes, the system demonstrates a viable pathway toward fast and practical single-image 3D reconstruction and manipulation, with potential applications in AR/VR content generation, digital heritage, e-commerce visualization, and interactive 3D content creation.

Challenge

Problem Statement

1

View Inconsistency & Janus Problem

Diffusion models trained on 2D datasets often exhibit viewpoint bias toward canonical orientations, leading to inconsistent multi-view reconstructions. This results in artifacts such as the "Janus effect," where objects appear with duplicated or distorted front-facing geometry.

2

Computational Inefficiency

Neural Radiance Fields (NeRFs) achieve high-quality photorealistic renderings but require hours of optimization and expensive volumetric ray marching, limiting their practical use in real-time or iterative applications.

3

Detail vs. Consistency Trade-off

Methods emphasizing fine visual detail via 2D priors often sacrifice geometric accuracy, while strongly regularized 3D methods yield smooth but less detailed outputs. Achieving both fine-grained detail and spatial consistency remains a key challenge.

4

Lack of Efficient Initialization

Many single-image pipelines rely on random or uniform sampling to initialize 3D representations, resulting in slow convergence and unstable optimization. Incorporating monocular depth priors can provide more accurate initialization.

5

Limited Post-Reconstruction Editing

Most 3D reconstruction pipelines produce static outputs that cannot be easily modified or edited according to user preferences. The lack of intuitive editing interfaces limits the practical utility of reconstructed 3D assets.

6

Slow Iterative Optimization

Traditional 3D editing approaches require per-scene optimization, making real-time interactive editing impractical. Feedforward models that can instantly generate or modify 3D content are needed.

Unified Reconstruction Pipeline

Implement a unified single-image 3D reconstruction pipeline that integrates monocular depth estimation, importance-sampled point cloud generation, and camera-conditioned score distillation sampling (CC-SDS).

Achieve geometrically consistent, high-fidelity reconstructions by leveraging pre-trained 2D diffusion models for viewpoint-aware optimization and detailed surface refinement.

Optimize for computational efficiency using 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS), enabling near real-time rendering and faster convergence compared to NeRF-based systems.

Feedforward 3D Editing

Compare the proposed editing framework against state-of-the-art methods such as SPARC3D to validate performance in terms of generation quality and computational efficiency.

Scope

Project Scope

A modular multi-stage architecture ensuring flexibility and scalability

Stage I

Geometric Inference

Extracting geometric priors from a single RGB image using monocular depth estimation and surface normal prediction. A dense 3D point cloud is generated through stratified importance sampling, concentrating sampling density in geometrically informative regions.

Stage II

Diffusion-Guided Optimization

The initialized point cloud is converted into a Gaussian representation and optimized using camera-conditioned score distillation sampling. 3D Gaussian Splatting enables real-time rendering and mesh extraction with baked textures.

Stage III

Feedforward Editing

A complementary feedforward pipeline for rapid 3D asset generation and direct editing. Enables text-to-3D generation, image-to-3D conversion, and text-guided editing of existing 3D assets without multi-view intermediate representations.
