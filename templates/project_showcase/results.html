Reconstruction

Reconstruction Pipeline Results

Geometric Accuracy

Camera-conditioned SDS effectively mitigated the Janus problem, resulting in geometrically consistent reconstructions across novel viewpoints.

  • Multi-view consistent geometry
  • Reduced artifacts significantly
  • Accurate depth estimation

Computational Efficiency

Replacing volumetric ray marching with Gaussian rasterization reduced training time by 60% while maintaining visual quality.

  • 60% faster training
  • Real-time rendering at 1080p
  • Lower memory footprint

Initialization Impact

Importance-sampled initialization proved especially critical, accelerating optimization and preventing degeneracy during early training.

  • 20% fewer iterations needed
  • More stable convergence
  • Better final quality
Editing

Feedforward Pipeline Results

Direct Asset Manipulation

Unlike multi-view approaches, the pipeline directly edits complete 3D representations, eliminating reconstruction artifacts and ensuring perfect geometric consistency.

Inference-Only Speed

Pure inference on pre-trained models enables generation in 20-30 seconds with no training or optimization required, making it 240× faster than iterative methods.

Predictable Performance

Inference-only operation ensures consistent generation times and quality across all inputs, unlike optimization-based methods whose convergence varies by scene complexity.

Instruction Processing

Qwen2509's ability to parse complex instructions and generate structured prompts significantly improved usability and enabled precise control over editing operations.

Limitations

Known Limitations

Reconstruction Pipeline
  • Per-scene optimization required (45+ min)
  • Foreground bias in initialization
  • Ambiguity in occluded areas
  • Quality depends on iteration count
Feedforward Pipeline
  • Detail loss from single-pass inference
  • Fixed model capabilities (no fine-tuning)
  • High memory requirements (>30GB)
  • Limited topological changes
System-Level
  • Dataset bias from training data
  • Requires A100 GPU (40GB+ VRAM)
  • Evaluation metrics are incomplete
  • Limited to single objects currently
Discussion

Key Findings

Integrated System Advantages

The combination of both optimization-based and feedforward pipelines creates a comprehensive framework with several key advantages:

1
Workflow Flexibility

Users can choose between rapid feedforward generation for initial prototypes and optimization-based refinement for final assets, depending on their needs.

2
Iterative Enhancement

Feedforward outputs can serve as initialization for optimization-based refinement, combining speed advantages with quality improvements.

3
Application-Specific Selection

Interactive applications benefit from feedforward speed, while archival reconstruction benefits from optimization quality—users can select the appropriate approach for each use case.
